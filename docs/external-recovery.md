# External Interruption and Recovery Contract

Status: normative readiness contract; implementation and release evidence are
still required. This document does not approve any external-use level.

This matrix defines restart behavior at every production transition seam used
by attended tasks, bounded queues, the foreground daemon, notification
delivery, and explicit archive administration. It applies after process exit,
forced termination, or machine interruption. Cancellation that returns only
after its owning operation has durably settled is an ordinary terminal outcome,
not an interruption.

## Rules shared by every seam

An automatic replay is allowed only when the same stable operation or delivery
ID reopens a complete, exact durable authority chain and current task, state,
workspace, Git, effective configuration, ledger, receipt, and artifact evidence
still agrees with it. Mutable checkpoints and journals are caches: they may be
missing or exactly behind their contiguous immutable history, but may never
lead or conflict with it. Replay reuses already-proven effects; it does not
infer them from filenames, prose, process exit, a clean worktree, or the
presence or absence of one artifact.

Exact replay never resumes a Codex session or a process. A model invocation may
start as a new fresh ephemeral process under the same durable operation only
when durable evidence proves that the invocation had not started. Once an
external process may have started, absence of complete accepted output is an
unprovable in-flight boundary.

When a row below says **quarantine**, Revolvr must append terminal
`unsafe_or_ambiguous` evidence to the old operation and preserve that operation
as immutable evidence. Level 1 stops the attended task and waits. Levels 2 and
3 must first persist task-scoped quarantine and exclude the unchanged task;
only then may they continue unrelated ready work in canonical order. Level 3
must also exclude the quarantined authority from daemon wake fingerprints.
Dependency- or conflict-related work remains blocked. No level may
automatically clear quarantine or resume ambiguous work.

The manual recovery operation is deliberately distinct from ordinary retry:

```text
revolvr task recover <task-id> --operation-id <old-operation-id>
revolvr task recover <task-id> --operation-id <old-operation-id> \
  --reconcile --confirm-operation <old-operation-id>
```

The first form is read-only. The second may create a new operation identity
only after task, state, workspace, Git, ledger, receipt, and artifact authority
all agree; it never rewrites or deletes the old operation. Until this command
and its production recovery proof exist, an ambiguous operation remains
stopped and external use remains unapproved. Editing `.revolvr`, task state,
history, ledger, receipts, or artifacts by hand is never recovery.

`task retry`, `task unblock`, rerunning an old task with changed material,
selecting it in a new queue, or waking the daemon are **generic retry**. Generic
retry cannot clear quarantine, replace recovery inspection, or convert
uncertainty into authority.

## Transition-seam matrix

Each row is independent. “L1/L2/L3” means Level 1 attended single task, Level 2
unattended sequential bounded queue, and Level 3 unattended foreground daemon.

## Evidence and release requirements

The old operation, every immutable history entry, partial artifact, retained
commit/ref, delivery attempt, queue slot, and archive transition remains
evidence until an explicit retention policy proves it is no longer needed by
active work, quarantine, recovery, receipts, completion, notification, or
archive verification. Recovery never means deleting inconvenient evidence.

Level 1 release evidence must prove task and explicit-administration restart at
every applicable row. Level 2 additionally must prove durable quarantine,
unchanged-task exclusion, sequential queue reconciliation, and unrelated-work
continuation. Level 3 additionally must prove no self-wake, bounded-sweep
restart, and identical recovery inside the foreground daemon. Passing a higher
row does not waive a lower readiness-level gate.
